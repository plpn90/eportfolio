Implementing Hardened Systems with ISO/IEC 27000

Safeguarding information assets extends beyond technological solutions; it necessitates the application of well-established principles and standards. The ISO/IEC 27000 series provides a comprehensive framework for the effective management of information security. However, it is critical to acknowledge that the weakest link of security is inherently the most vulnerable component - the human factor. With this context, I selected some key concepts which are helpful to mitigate the risks associated with human error.

Access control is designed to ensure that only individuals with proper authorisation can access specific systems or data, thereby minimising the risk of both unintentional and intentional breaches. For example, role-based access control assigns permissions based on specific job responsibilities, ensuring that users only have access to the information necessary for their roles. Role-based access control also offers another advantage: it reduces the likelihood of errors during on- and off-boarding personal. This is because all the granted permissions are readily accessible in a single location, making it less likely that an ex-employee retains access beyond their employment period (Sutton 2022).

Authentication complements access control by verifying the identity of users attempting to gain access to systems. Common methods include passwords, biometric verification, and multi-factor authentication (MFA). Implementing strong authentication protocols significantly reduces the risk of unauthorised access and identity impersonation.

Auditing every action is essential for maintaining traceability and accountability within an organisation. By logging user activities and access events, organisations can detect irregularities and perform investigations of security incidents (Sutton 2022). Regular audits also contribute to the continuous improvement of the Information Security Management System (ISMS).

Confidentiality is a core principle of information security, focusing on restricting data access to authorised personnel only. Techniques such as data encryption, secure communication protocols, and the enforcement of non-disclosure agreements are commonly employed to uphold data confidentiality (Mutch et al 2011).

Lastly, security implementation standards provide a set of guidelines and controls necessary for translating security policies into actionable measures. Standards based on ISO/IEC 27001, supported by ISO/IEC 27002, help organisations establish measurable, repeatable processes that can be systematically audited and refined for continuous enhancement.
